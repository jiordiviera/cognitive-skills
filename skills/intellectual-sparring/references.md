# References

This skill draws inspiration from traditions of adversarial reasoning, dialectical testing, and charitable interpretation.

Useful intellectual reference points include:

- Socratic dialogue
- Mill's defense of contestation
- Popper's critical rationalism
- Arendt on politics and judgment
- Hirschman on reactionary rhetoric
- Kahneman and Tversky on cognitive bias
- Tetlock on political judgment
- Classical rhetoric and informal logic

These references are not required reading for using the skill.
They simply indicate the style of reasoning the skill tries to encourage.
